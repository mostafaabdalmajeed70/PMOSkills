# PD05 — Project Work Performance Domain
**Type:** Performance Domain
**PMBOK8 Source:** PMBOK8 Guide §2.5 — Project Work Performance Domain
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
The Project Work Performance Domain encompasses the activities and functions associated with establishing project processes, managing physical resources, and fostering a learning environment (PMBOK8 §2.5). This domain covers the operational dimension of project management — the day-to-day execution work that keeps the project running. It is distinct from product or deliverable scope (which belongs to the Delivery domain); rather, it concerns the management of project processes themselves: how work is organized, how information flows, how resources are obtained and used, how knowledge is preserved, and how the project team maintains the conditions under which productive work can occur.

## Desired Outcomes
- Efficient and effective project performance where processes are appropriately tailored to the project context
- Productive working environment in which team members can perform their assigned work and resolve issues quickly
- Appropriate management and use of physical and material resources throughout the project
- Effective procurement and supplier relationships that deliver contracted value on time
- Organizational learning is supported through systematic capture and transfer of knowledge and lessons

## Key Activities and Considerations
- **Establish project processes:** Define and implement processes that are appropriate to the project — tailored to scale, complexity, and methodology — and ensure the team understands how work will flow through the project
- **Manage project knowledge and information:** Facilitate the capture, organization, and sharing of knowledge — both explicit (documented) and tacit (experiential) — so that lessons are not lost and information is accessible to those who need it
- **Manage material and physical resources:** Identify resource needs, acquire materials and equipment, monitor usage and availability, and take corrective action when resource shortfalls or surpluses occur
- **Manage procurement:** Plan and conduct procurement activities — including vendor selection, contract management, and supplier performance monitoring — ensuring that procured goods and services meet project requirements
- **Maintain project communications:** Ensure that the right information reaches the right stakeholders at the right time, using communication methods and channels appropriate to the project context
- **Monitor work performance:** Continuously observe project execution against plans, identify deviations and impediments, and take timely corrective or preventive actions
- **Foster a learning environment:** Create conditions where team members can experiment, reflect, and improve; support retrospectives, lessons learned sessions, and knowledge transfer activities

## Questions to Ask in Practice
1. Are the project processes appropriately tailored — not too heavy for a small project, not too lean for a complex one?
2. Is knowledge being captured and made accessible, or is critical information locked in individual team members' heads?
3. Are procurement and supplier relationships being actively managed, or are contracts treated as set-and-forget?
4. When work impediments arise, is there a clear path to resolution, and are actions tracked through to completion?
