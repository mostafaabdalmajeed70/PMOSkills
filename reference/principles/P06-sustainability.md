# P06 — Integrate Sustainability Within All Project Areas
**Type:** Principle
**PMBOK8 Source:** PMBOK8 Standard §3.6 — Integrate Sustainability Within All Project Areas
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Sustainability within project management, as defined in PMBOK 8 Standard §3.6, requires project practitioners to actively integrate environmental, social, and economic dimensions into all aspects of project delivery. A sustainable approach ensures that the project's outputs, outcomes, and delivery processes do not compromise the ability of future generations to meet their needs. This principle demands that project teams balance short-term progress with long-term ecological balance, community well-being, and ethical governance. Sustainability is not a separate workstream but a core lens applied across all planning, resource acquisition, risk management, and value delivery activities.

## Key Characteristics
- **Environmental Stewardship:** Teams actively work to minimize the project's carbon footprint, optimize resource use, and eliminate waste through circular economy principles (§3.6).
- **Social Equity:** Projects must respect human rights, promote fair labor standards, ensure worker safety, and positive community relations (§3.6).
- **Economic Viability:** Financial models include life-cycle costing, post-project maintenance, and long-term economic ROI alongside short-term project budget boundaries (§3.6).
- **Integrated Decision-Making:** Sustainability objectives are evaluated concurrently with scope, schedule, cost, and quality during change control reviews (§3.6).
- **Future-Proofing Outcomes:** Deliverables are designed for resilience, adaptability, and sustainable decommissioning at the end of their lifecycle (§3.6).

## Questions to Ask in Practice
1. Does our project plan actively minimize carbon emissions, water use, and material waste?
2. Have we evaluated our suppliers and subcontractors against strict ESG and fair labor standards?
3. How will the outputs of this project be decommissioned or recycled at the end of their lifecycle?
4. Have we checked for potential negative social impacts on local communities or stakeholder groups?
5. Are we considering long-term lifecycle maintenance costs, or just optimizing for immediate project budget limits?
