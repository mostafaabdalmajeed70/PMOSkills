# P01 — Be a Diligent, Respectful, and Caring Steward
**Type:** Principle
**PMBOK8 Source:** PMBOK8 Standard §3.1 — Be a Diligent, Respectful, and Caring Steward
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Stewardship, as defined in PMBOK 8 Standard §3.1, means acting as a trustworthy and responsible caretaker of the resources, relationships, and outcomes entrusted to the project practitioner. Stewards are diligent in their work, respectful to all people involved or affected, and caring about the broader impacts of their decisions. This principle extends accountability beyond the project team to encompass organizational, community, and environmental consequences. Stewardship is holistic — covering financial, material, human, and natural resources — and is grounded in integrity, compliance, and sustainability consciousness.

## Key Characteristics
- **Holistic resource responsibility:** Stewards manage financial, material, human, and natural resources with care, avoiding waste and ensuring resources are used in alignment with organizational intent (§3.1).
- **Integrity and accountability:** Practitioners uphold ethical standards and accept accountability for decisions and actions, even when outcomes are uncertain or adverse (§3.1).
- **Transparency in reporting:** Stewardship requires honest, timely reporting of project performance, risks, and issues — not just positive information (§3.1).
- **Sustainability consciousness:** Stewards consider the short- and long-term environmental and societal impacts of project decisions, not only internal project metrics (§3.1).
- **Organizational and external awareness:** Stewardship extends to the organization and its external stakeholders, communities, and environment — recognizing that projects have impacts beyond their immediate deliverables (§3.1).
- **Compliance orientation:** Stewards ensure that project work complies with applicable laws, regulations, organizational policies, and professional standards (§3.1).

## Questions to Ask in Practice
1. Am I being fully transparent with stakeholders about the project's performance, including problems and risks, or am I managing perceptions?
2. Are the resources under my oversight being used efficiently and in alignment with the organizational intent stated in the Business Case?
3. Have I considered the environmental and community impacts of this project's decisions, not just the internal project outcomes?
4. Am I complying with all applicable laws, regulations, organizational policies, and professional standards — and can I demonstrate that compliance if asked?
5. If something goes wrong with this project, would I be comfortable being fully accountable for the decisions and actions I have taken?
