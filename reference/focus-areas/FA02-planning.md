# FA02 — Planning
**Type:** Focus Area
**PMBOK8 Source:** Process Groups Practice Guide — Planning
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Planning is the focus area in which the total scope of effort is established, all project objectives are detailed, and the integrated course of action for achieving those objectives is developed. Planning produces the baselines — scope, schedule, and financial — against which project performance will be measured throughout execution and monitoring. It also produces all subsidiary management plans that govern how specific knowledge areas (quality, risk, communications, procurement, stakeholder engagement) will be managed. Planning is iterative: plans are progressively elaborated and may be revisited as new information emerges, but the transition to execution requires an approved, integrated plan.

## Entry Conditions
- Project Charter is approved and signed
- Project Manager is formally assigned with defined authority
- Initial Stakeholder Register from FA01 is available
- Sponsor and key stakeholders are available to participate in requirements gathering and scope definition
- Organizational process assets and enterprise environmental factors have been documented in the Context Register (A05)

## Primary Activities
- Develop the Integrated Project Management Plan that integrates all subsidiary plans
- Define and decompose scope; create the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
- Collect, analyze, and document requirements; build the Requirements Traceability Matrix
- Develop the Schedule Baseline using sequencing, resource estimation, and duration analysis
- Develop the Financial Baseline through cost estimation and budget authorization
- Plan quality and define quality standards, metrics, and acceptance criteria
- Plan communications and establish the communications matrix
- Plan stakeholder engagement and develop targeted engagement strategies
- Plan risk management; identify, analyze (qualitative and quantitative), and plan risk responses
- Plan procurement; determine make-or-buy decisions and prepare procurement documentation
- Develop the Change Management Plan to govern benefit realization and organizational change
- Tailor the project management approach to the project's context, complexity, and delivery method
- Document team operating norms and resource capacity

## Relationship to Performance Domains
Planning is the most multi-domain focus area; it directly engages or sets the foundation for nearly every performance domain:

- **PD04 — Planning:** The primary domain for this focus area; all planning processes, tools, baselines, and subsidiary plans are produced here
- **PD03 — Development Approach and Life Cycle:** The delivery approach (predictive, adaptive, hybrid) is finalized and tailored; phase gating and iteration cadence are defined
- **PD08 — Uncertainty:** Risk management planning, risk identification, and risk analysis are conducted; the risk register and reserve strategy are established
- **PD01 — Stakeholders:** Stakeholder engagement strategies are refined; communications planning directly serves stakeholder needs
- **PD02 — Team:** Resource planning, team structure, roles, responsibilities, and operating agreements are defined
- **PD05 — Project Work:** Work packages, activity sequences, and resource assignments create the foundation for directing project work in FA03
- **PD07 — Measurement:** Performance measurement baselines (scope, schedule, cost) are established; KPIs and reporting thresholds are defined
- **PD06 — Delivery:** Acceptance criteria, quality standards, and the RTM are defined; the delivery roadmap is articulated

## Exit Criteria / Quality Gate
- Integrated Project Management Plan is reviewed and formally approved by the sponsor
- All three baselines (scope, schedule, financial) are approved and baselined
- All subsidiary management plans are complete and consistent with the integrated plan
- Risk management record contains identified and analyzed risks with documented response strategies
- RTM is populated and traceable from requirements to deliverables
- Tailoring decisions are documented and approved
- Planning complete gate is formally passed with sponsor authorization to proceed to execution
