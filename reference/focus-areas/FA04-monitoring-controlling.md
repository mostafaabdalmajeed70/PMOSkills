# FA04 — Monitoring and Controlling
**Type:** Focus Area
**PMBOK8 Source:** Process Groups Practice Guide — Monitoring and Controlling
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Monitoring and Controlling is the focus area that tracks, reviews, and regulates the progress and performance of the project against the approved baselines. It identifies any areas in which changes to the plan are required and initiates the corresponding corrective, preventive, or change-control actions. Unlike the other focus areas, Monitoring and Controlling does not occur at a single point in the lifecycle — it runs concurrently with all other focus areas from project initiation through closure. This continuous nature makes it the feedback and governance engine of the project, ensuring that deviations are detected early, decisions are made with current data, and the project remains aligned with authorized scope, schedule, cost, and quality targets.

## Entry Conditions
- Project execution (FA03) has begun and work is producing measurable outputs
- Performance baselines (scope, schedule, financial) are approved and established
- Performance reporting cadence and thresholds are defined in the Integrated PMP (A14)
- A change control process and Change Log (A12) are in place
- Risk monitoring responsibilities are assigned

## Primary Activities
- Monitor and control project work by collecting performance data, analyzing variances, and reporting status against all baselines
- Perform integrated change control by reviewing all change requests, assessing impacts, and approving or rejecting changes through the change control board or designated authority
- Control scope by comparing delivered scope against the scope baseline and managing scope creep
- Control schedule by analyzing schedule performance indices, identifying critical path impacts, and initiating corrective actions
- Control costs by analyzing cost performance indices, earned value metrics, and financial forecast variances
- Control quality by inspecting deliverables against acceptance criteria, performing testing, and managing defect resolution
- Monitor stakeholder engagement by assessing whether stakeholder satisfaction and engagement levels remain aligned with the communications plan
- Monitor risks by reviewing the Risk Management Record, tracking risk triggers, implementing contingency plans, and identifying new risks
- Control procurements by reviewing vendor performance against contract terms, managing claims, and overseeing contract changes

| Artifact | Purpose |
|---|---|
| A17 — Performance Reporting Record (updated each cycle) | Records current performance status, variance analysis, and forecasts; the primary communication vehicle for project health |
| A12 — Change Log (change decisions recorded) | Documents all change requests, impact assessments, approval/rejection decisions, and implementation status |
| A18 — Issue Log (updated) | Tracks open issues identified through monitoring, their resolution actions, owners, and closure status |
| A19 — Risk Management Record (risk response tracking) | Updated with risk status, trigger observations, response execution records, and newly identified risks |
| A20 — Quantitative Risk Reserve Decision Record (reserve drawdowns) | Records decisions to draw down contingency or management reserves and the justification for each drawdown |

## Relationship to Performance Domains
Monitoring and Controlling is the governance overlay that connects all performance domains through measurement and feedback:

- **PD07 — Measurement:** The primary domain; all performance measurement, KPI tracking, earned value analysis, variance reporting, and forecasting occur here; this domain exists almost entirely within this focus area
- **PD08 — Uncertainty:** Risk monitoring, trigger identification, contingency drawdowns, and reserve management keep the project's uncertainty posture current and controlled
- **PD06 — Delivery:** Scope validation, quality inspection, and acceptance reviews ensure deliverables meet defined standards before formal acceptance
- **PD04 — Planning:** Approved changes from integrated change control feed back into plan updates; re-baselining decisions occur here when authorized
- **PD01 — Stakeholders:** Stakeholder engagement monitoring identifies satisfaction gaps; corrective communications actions are initiated
- **PD05 — Project Work:** Work performance data is the primary input from FA03 Executing; control decisions influence work direction in real time

## Exit Criteria / Quality Gate
- All planned deliverables are formally accepted by the sponsor or designated acceptance authority
- All performance variances are within approved tolerance bands or have approved corrective action plans
- No open critical issues remain in the Issue Log (A18)
- All change requests are resolved (approved, rejected, or deferred) with documented decisions in the Change Log (A12)
- Risk posture is documented as acceptable; all critical risks are closed or have active response plans
- Final performance reports are current and complete
- Closure is formally authorized by the sponsor; transition to FA05 Closing is approved
