# FA03 — Executing
**Type:** Focus Area
**PMBOK8 Source:** Process Groups Practice Guide — Executing
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Executing is the focus area in which the work defined in the approved Integrated Project Management Plan is directed, coordinated, and performed to produce the project deliverables. This focus area is where the plan becomes reality: the team is developed and managed, stakeholders are actively engaged, procurements are conducted, quality assurance activities are implemented, and project knowledge is captured and shared. Executing is the most resource-intensive focus area and operates in close, continuous feedback with FA04 Monitoring and Controlling to ensure that work remains aligned with approved plans and baselines.

## Entry Conditions
- Integrated Project Management Plan is formally approved by the sponsor
- All three baselines (scope, schedule, financial) are established and approved
- Project resources have been acquired or are committed and available
- Team operating agreements and role assignments from FA02 are in place
- Procurement vehicles (contracts, agreements) are executed or in process
- Kickoff meeting has been conducted and team is mobilized

## Primary Activities
- Direct and manage project work by authorizing work packages, coordinating team activities, and producing deliverables according to the schedule and scope baseline
- Manage project knowledge by capturing lessons learned, sharing expertise, and updating the Knowledge Record continuously
- Develop and manage the project team through coaching, conflict resolution, performance management, and skill development
- Manage stakeholder engagement by executing communication activities per the Communications Management Plan and responding to stakeholder needs and concerns
- Conduct procurements by executing vendor selection processes, awarding contracts, and managing vendor onboarding
- Manage quality by performing quality assurance activities, process audits, and ensuring deliverables meet defined acceptance criteria before submission
- Manage communications by producing, distributing, and archiving project information per the Communications Management Plan
- Implement change management activities from the Change Management Plan to support organizational readiness and benefit realization

## Relationship to Performance Domains
Executing is the focus area in which the most performance domains are simultaneously active and producing value:

- **PD05 — Project Work:** The primary domain; all work authorization, coordination, and delivery activity occurs here; the project management information system is in active use
- **PD06 — Delivery:** Deliverables are produced and undergo quality checks; scope is progressively realized
- **PD01 — Stakeholders:** Stakeholder engagement is actively managed; communications are executed; satisfaction is monitored and adjusted
- **PD02 — Team:** Team development, performance management, and conflict resolution are the core people activities of this focus area
- **PD03 — Development Approach:** The chosen delivery approach (predictive sprints, iterations, or hybrid) governs how work is sequenced and delivered
- **PD08 — Uncertainty:** Risk responses are implemented; emerging risks are identified and escalated to FA04 for control action
- **PD04 — Planning:** Re-planning and rolling wave planning occur as progressive elaboration reveals new information; plan updates are submitted as change requests
- **PD07 — Measurement:** Performance data is collected and reported; actual vs. baseline metrics are generated for FA04 consumption

## Exit Criteria / Quality Gate
- All planned deliverables have been produced and are available for formal acceptance review
- Deliverables meet defined quality standards and acceptance criteria documented in the RTM (A13)
- No unresolved blocking issues remain in the Issue Log (A18)
- All approved change requests have been implemented or are formally deferred
- Knowledge Record (A30) reflects all material lessons learned from execution
- Performance data has been reported and is current in the Performance Reporting Record (A17)
- Sponsor or designated acceptance authority confirms readiness for closure review
