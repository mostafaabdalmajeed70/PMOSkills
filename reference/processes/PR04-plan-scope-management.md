# PR04 — Plan Scope Management
**Type:** Process
**Process Group:** Planning
**Knowledge Area:** Project Scope Management
**PMBOK8 Source:** Process Groups: A Practice Guide § 3.2
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Plan Scope Management is the process of creating a scope management plan that documents how the project and product scope will be defined, validated, and controlled (PG:APG § 3.2). It produces the governing policy for all subsequent scope processes — setting the rules and responsibilities for scope decisions before requirements gathering begins.

## Purpose
To establish the governance framework for scope work: who has authority to define scope, how changes are handled, and what criteria will be used to accept deliverables.

## Key Outputs
| Output | Description |
|---|---|
| Scope Management Plan | Subsidiary plan defining how scope will be defined, validated, and controlled |
| Requirements Management Plan | Subsidiary plan for how requirements will be collected, tracked, and managed |

## Key Characteristics
- **Governance-first:** This process establishes the rules before any scope content is defined, preventing ad hoc scope decisions later (PG:APG § 3.2).
- **Two-plan output:** Uniquely produces both a Scope Management Plan and a Requirements Management Plan — separating scope governance from requirements governance (PG:APG § 3.2).
- **Context-sensitive:** The depth of these plans is tailored based on project complexity, regulatory requirements, and organizational standards (PG:APG § 3.2).

## Questions to Ask in Practice
1. Have scope authority boundaries been defined — who can approve scope changes at each threshold level?
2. Does the Requirements Management Plan define traceability requirements from requirements through to deliverables?
3. Are the validation criteria for deliverable acceptance objective and measurable?
