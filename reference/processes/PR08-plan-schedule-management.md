# PR08 — Plan Schedule Management
**Type:** Process
**Process Group:** Planning
**Knowledge Area:** Project Schedule Management
**PMBOK8 Source:** Process Groups: A Practice Guide § 3.6
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Plan Schedule Management is the process of establishing the policies, procedures, and documentation for planning, developing, managing, executing, and controlling the project schedule (PG:APG § 3.6). It produces the Schedule Management Plan — the subsidiary plan that governs how all subsequent schedule processes will be conducted, including how schedule variances will be addressed and what thresholds will trigger corrective action.

## Purpose
To define the rules, tools, and governance for schedule management before any scheduling work begins, preventing ad hoc schedule decisions and ensuring consistency across the project lifecycle.

| Output | Description |

| Schedule Management Plan | Subsidiary plan defining scheduling methodology, tools, level of accuracy, variance thresholds, and update frequency |

## Key Characteristics
- **Governance-first:** Establishes schedule rules before activities are defined, preventing inconsistent schedule management practices (PG:APG § 3.6).
- **Methodology selection:** The plan specifies the scheduling methodology to be used — CPM, rolling wave, agile sprints, or hybrid (PG:APG § 3.6, PSS-3E).
- **Threshold definition:** Variance thresholds and escalation triggers for schedule deviations are defined here, not during monitoring (PG:APG § 3.6).
- **Update cadence:** The plan establishes how often the schedule is updated and who is responsible for updates (PG:APG § 3.6).

## Questions to Ask in Practice
1. Has the scheduling methodology been chosen based on the project’s delivery approach and complexity, rather than defaulting to the organization’s standard tool?
2. Are schedule variance thresholds defined numerically — e.g., SPI < 0.9 triggers escalation — rather than left to judgment?
3. Does the plan specify who is accountable for schedule updates and at what frequency?
