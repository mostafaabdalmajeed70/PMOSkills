# PR09 — Define Activities

**Type:** Process Record

**Process Group:** Planning  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Active

## Definition

Define Activities is the process of identifying and documenting the specific actions to be performed to produce the project deliverables.

## Purpose

This process decomposes WBS work packages into the discrete schedule activities required to produce deliverables. It translates the scope baseline into executable units of work that can be sequenced, assigned resources, estimated for duration, and tracked during execution. This process may recur throughout the life cycle in rolling-wave and adaptive projects as planning detail increases.

## When Used

This process follows Create WBS (PR07) and is performed during Planning. For adaptive or rolling-wave projects, activity definition continues iteratively as upcoming scope is elaborated into planned work.

## Key Inputs

- Project management plan, especially the schedule management plan (from PR08) and scope baseline (from PR07).
- Enterprise environmental factors.
- Organizational process assets.

- Expert judgment.

## Key Outputs

- Activity list.
- Activity attributes.
- Milestone list.
- Change requests.
- Project management plan updates.

## Relationship to Other Processes

- Relies on Create WBS (PR07) for work packages that are the starting point for activity decomposition.
- Relies on Plan Schedule Management (PR08) for the scheduling rules and methodology that govern activity definition.
- Directly feeds Sequence Activities (PR10), which establishes logical relationships among the defined activities.
- Also feeds Estimate Activity Durations and Estimate Activity Resources, which require a defined activity list to function.

## Artifact Connections

- **A15 Schedule Baseline:** the activity list is the input from which the schedule network and ultimately the schedule baseline are built.
- **A14 Integrated Project Management Plan:** schedule management plan updates may result from activity definition decisions.
- **A08 Scope and Requirements Backlog:** in adaptive projects, the activity list corresponds to the user stories and tasks within a sprint or iteration backlog.
- **A05 Context Register:** constraints and assumptions that limit or shape how work can be decomposed into activities.

## Practitioner Questions

- Is every work package from the WBS represented by one or more activities in the activity list?
- Are activities defined at a level of detail that supports meaningful duration estimating and resource assignment?
- Have milestones been identified to mark significant points, phase boundaries, or approval gates?
- How does rolling-wave planning affect activity definition in the near term versus longer-horizon work?
- Are activity attributes sufficient to support sequencing, dependency analysis, and schedule network construction?
