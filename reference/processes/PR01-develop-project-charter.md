# PR01 — Develop Project Charter

**Type:** Process Record  
**Primary Source:** PMBOK 8 — Governance Performance Domain, 2.1.6.1 Initiate Project or Phase  
**Companion Source:** Process Groups: A Practice Guide — 4.1 Develop Project Charter  
**Process Group:** Initiating  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Active

## Definition

Develop Project Charter is the process of developing the document that formally authorizes the existence of a project and provides the project manager with the authority to apply organizational resources to project activities.

## Purpose

This process creates the formal authorization to begin project work and establishes a direct link between the project, the business case, and organizational strategic objectives. It also gives the project manager recognized authority before detailed planning begins.

## When Used

This process is performed once or at predefined points in the project or phase life cycle. In phased work, the initiating information may be revisited at the start of later phases to confirm continuing business alignment.

## Key Inputs

- Business documents, especially the business case and benefits management plan.
- Agreements, where relevant to customer or supplier arrangements.
- Enterprise environmental factors.
- Organizational process assets.

## Key Tools and Techniques

- Expert judgment.
- Data gathering, including brainstorming, focus groups, and interviews.
- Interpersonal and team skills, especially facilitation, conflict management, and meeting management.
- Meetings.

## Key Outputs

- Project charter.
- Assumption log.

## Relationship to Other Processes

- Enables Identify Stakeholders (PR02) by supplying the initial authority context, purpose, and high-level stakeholder framing.
- Feeds the major planning processes, including plan scope, schedule, cost, quality, resource, communications, risk, procurement, stakeholder engagement, and integrated planning.
- Provides an authorization point later referenced during Close Project or Phase.

## Practitioner Questions

- What business need, opportunity, regulatory requirement, or strategic objective is this project intended to address?
- Has the project manager been assigned with clear authority before planning starts?
- Are the expected benefits, success criteria, and high-level boundaries explicit enough to support planning?
- Are the sponsor and authorizing authority at the correct level to commit funding and resources?
- What assumptions are being accepted at charter approval time?
