# PR24 — Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis

## Classification
- **Performance Domain:** Risk
- **Process Group:** Planning (not required for all projects)
- **PMBOK 8 Reference:** Section 2.7.2.3
- **Process Groups Practice Guide Reference:** Section 5.21

---

## Purpose
Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis is the process of numerically analyzing the combined effect of identified individual project risks and other sources of uncertainty on overall project objectives. The key benefit is that it quantifies overall project risk exposure and provides additional quantitative risk information to support risk response planning.

---

## Inputs
- Project management plan (risk management plan, scope baseline, schedule baseline, cost baseline)
- Project documents: assumption log, basis of estimates, cost estimates, cost forecasts, duration estimates, milestone list, resource requirements, risk register, risk report, schedule forecasts
- Enterprise environmental factors
- Organizational process assets

---

## Tools & Techniques
- Expert judgment
- Data gathering (interviews)
- Interpersonal and team skills (facilitation)
- Representations of uncertainty (probability distributions: triangular, normal, lognormal, beta, uniform, discrete)
- Data analysis: simulations (Monte Carlo), sensitivity analysis (tornado diagram), decision tree analysis, influence diagrams

---

## Outputs
- Project documents updates (risk report)

---

## Key Concepts
- Not required for all projects; used most for large, complex, or strategically important projects.
- Monte Carlo simulation calculates multiple project duration/cost outcomes using probability distributions.
- Tornado diagram shows which individual risks have the most potential impact on project outcomes.
- Quantitative risk analysis is the only reliable method to assess overall project risk through evaluating the aggregated effect of all individual risks.
- Outputs feed directly into Plan Risk Responses.
