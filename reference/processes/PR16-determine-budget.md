# PR16 — Determine Budget

## Classification
- **Performance Domain:** Finance
- **Process Group:** Planning
- **PMBOK 8 Reference:** Section 2.4
- **Process Groups Practice Guide Reference:** Section 5.13

---

## Purpose
Determine Budget is the process of aggregating the estimated costs of individual activities or work packages to establish an authorized cost baseline. The key benefit is that it determines the cost baseline against which project performance can be monitored and controlled.

---

## Inputs
- Project management plan (cost management plan, resource management plan, scope baseline)
- Project documents: basis of estimates, cost estimates, project schedule, risk register
- Business documents (business case, benefits management plan)
- Agreements
- Enterprise environmental factors
- Organizational process assets

---

## Tools & Techniques
- Expert judgment
- Cost aggregation
- Data analysis (reserve analysis)
- Historical information review
- Funding limit reconciliation
- Financing

---

## Outputs
- Cost baseline
- Project funding requirements
- Project documents updates (cost estimates, project schedule, risk register)

---

## Key Concepts
- The project budget includes all funds authorized to execute the project.
- The cost baseline is the approved, time-phased budget excluding management reserves; it includes contingency reserves (in one scenario).
- Two budget buildup scenarios exist: one including contingency reserve in the cost baseline, one excluding it.
- Management reserves cover unknown-unknowns and are held outside the cost baseline.
- Funding limit reconciliation aligns cash flow with organizational funding constraints.
