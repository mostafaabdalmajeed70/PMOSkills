# PR14 — Plan Cost Management

## Classification
- **Performance Domain:** Finance
- **Process Group:** Planning
- **PMBOK 8 Reference:** Section 2.4
- **Process Groups Practice Guide Reference:** Section 5.11

---

## Purpose
Plan Cost Management is the process of defining how the project costs will be estimated, budgeted, managed, monitored, and controlled. The key benefit is that it provides guidance and direction on how project costs will be managed throughout the project.

---

## Inputs
- Project charter
- Project management plan (schedule management plan, risk management plan)
- Enterprise environmental factors
- Organizational process assets

---

## Tools & Techniques
- Expert judgment
- Data analysis (alternatives analysis)
- Meetings

---

## Outputs
- Cost management plan

---

## Key Concepts
- Cost management planning occurs early in project planning and sets the framework for all cost management processes.
- The cost management plan defines: units of measure, level of precision, level of accuracy, organizational procedures links (WBS/control accounts), control thresholds, EVM rules of performance measurement, and reporting formats.
- CapEx vs. OpEx distinction is relevant for financial constraint planning.
- Budget can be allocated quarterly in agile approaches for flexibility.
