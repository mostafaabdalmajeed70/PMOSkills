# PR05 — Collect Requirements

**Type:** Process Record  
**Primary Source:** PMBOK 8 — Scope Performance Domain, 2.2.2.2 Elicit and Analyze Requirements  
**Companion Source:** Process Groups: A Practice Guide — 5.3 Collect Requirements  
**Process Group:** Planning  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Active

## Definition

Collect Requirements is the process of determining, documenting, and managing stakeholder needs and requirements to meet project objectives.

## Purpose

This process transforms stakeholder needs and expectations into documented, traceable requirements that define the product, service, or result the project will deliver. It provides the foundation for scope definition, WBS construction, and quality planning. In adaptive environments, requirements are collected as user stories prioritized in a product backlog.

## When Used

This process is performed periodically throughout the project. It is most intensive during early planning but continues as stakeholders provide refined input, especially in iterative and adaptive approaches.

## Key Inputs

- Project charter.
- Project management plan components, especially the scope management plan and requirements management plan (from PR04).
- Stakeholder register (from PR02).
- Business documents, including business case and agreements.
- Assumption log, lessons learned register.
- Enterprise environmental factors.
- Organizational process assets.

## Key Tools and Techniques

- Expert judgment.
- Data gathering, including interviews, focus groups, questionnaires, surveys, and brainstorming.
- Data analysis, including document analysis.
- Decision-making techniques, including nominal group and voting.
- Data representation.
- Interpersonal and team skills.
- Design thinking and prioritization/ranking.
- Meetings.

## Key Outputs

- Requirements documentation.
- Requirements traceability matrix.

## Relationship to Other Processes

- Relies on Plan Scope Management (PR04) for the requirements management approach.
- Relies on Identify Stakeholders (PR02) for the stakeholder register needed to source requirements.
- Directly feeds Define Scope (PR06) and Create WBS / Develop Scope Structure (PR07).
- Supports Plan Quality Management and risk identification by clarifying expected performance and quality thresholds.
- Requirements documentation flows into Validate Scope and Monitor and Control Scope.

## Artifact Connections

- **A08 Scope and Requirements Backlog:** primary container for collected requirements in adaptive and hybrid projects.
- **A13 Requirements Traceability Matrix:** principal traceability output, connecting requirements to objectives, deliverables, and test cases.
- **A07 Stakeholder Register:** source of stakeholder-specific needs and engagement context.
- **A04 Project Charter:** source of high-level requirements and business objectives used to frame elicitation.
- **A14 Integrated Project Management Plan:** receives requirements management plan updates when requirements evolve.

## Skill Connections

## Domain and Focus Area Links

- Strongest PMBOK 8 alignment: Scope Performance Domain.
- Secondary alignment: Stakeholders, because requirements elicitation depends directly on stakeholder engagement quality.
- Strongest focus area alignment: Planning.

## Practitioner Questions

- Have all relevant stakeholders been included as requirement sources?
- Are requirements documented with sufficient specificity to be measurable and testable?
- How are conflicting stakeholder requirements being resolved and prioritized?
- Are requirements traceable from business objectives through to deliverables and acceptance criteria?
- How will requirements be maintained and updated as the project evolves?
