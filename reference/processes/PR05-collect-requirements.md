# PR05 — Collect Requirements
**Type:** Process
**Process Group:** Planning
**Knowledge Area:** Project Scope Management
**PMBOK8 Source:** Process Groups: A Practice Guide § 3.3
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Definition
Collect Requirements is the process of determining, documenting, and managing stakeholder needs and requirements to meet project and product objectives (PG:APG § 3.3). Requirements include the quantified and documented needs and expectations of the sponsor, customer, and other stakeholders. They form the basis of the WBS and all subsequent scope, schedule, and cost planning.

## Purpose
To establish a complete, traceable, and agreed-upon set of requirements that accurately reflects stakeholder needs, serving as the foundation for scope definition and deliverable design.

## Key Tools and Techniques
| Tool / Technique | Description |
|---|---|
| Expert judgment | Domain expertise to identify completeness of requirements |
| Data gathering (brainstorming, interviews, focus groups, questionnaires, benchmarking) | Active elicitation from stakeholders |
| Data analysis (document analysis) | Systematic review of existing documentation |
| Decision making (voting, autocratic, multicriteria analysis) | Resolving conflicting requirements |
| Data representation (affinity diagrams, mind mapping) | Organizing and categorizing requirements |
| Interpersonal and team skills (nominal group technique, observation, facilitation) | Working collaboratively to surface hidden requirements |
| Context diagram | Showing product scope visually — product boundary and interactions |
| Prototypes | Tangible models to elicit feedback from stakeholders |

## Key Outputs
| Output | Description |
|---|---|
| Requirements Documentation | Detailed record of how individual requirements meet business needs |
| Requirements Traceability Matrix (RTM) | Links requirements to objectives, deliverables, and test cases |

## Key Characteristics
- **Stakeholder-driven:** Requirements reflect stakeholder needs, not the PM's assumptions — elicitation quality directly determines planning quality (PG:APG § 3.3).
- **Traceability-mandatory:** Every requirement must be traceable from its source stakeholder through to deliverables and validation criteria (PG:APG § 3.3).
- **Iterative in adaptive delivery:** In agile or hybrid contexts, requirements are continuously refined throughout the project and managed in a backlog (PG:APG § 3.3).
- **Conflict resolution required:** Stakeholders often have conflicting requirements; this process includes structured techniques for resolving conflicts before requirements are baselined (PG:APG § 3.3).

## Questions to Ask in Practice
1. Have I elicited requirements from all identified stakeholders, including those who are hard to reach or who may be resistant?
2. Is every requirement testable — can I define an objective acceptance criterion for each one?
3. Have conflicting requirements been explicitly resolved and the resolution documented and agreed to by relevant stakeholders?
4. Is the RTM maintained such that any change to a requirement triggers a review of all linked deliverables and test cases?
5. In adaptive delivery, is the backlog being continuously refined and prioritized with stakeholder input rather than being treated as fixed?
