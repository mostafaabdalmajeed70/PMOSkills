# PR18 — Plan Resource Management

## Classification
- **Performance Domain:** Resources
- **Process Group:** Planning
- **PMBOK 8 Reference:** Section 2.6
- **Process Groups Practice Guide Reference:** Section 5.15 (implied)

---

## Purpose
Plan Resource Management is the process of defining how to estimate, acquire, manage, and utilize physical and team resources. The key benefit is that it establishes the approach and level of management effort needed for managing project resources based on the type and complexity of the project.

---

## Inputs
- Project charter
- Project management plan (quality management plan, scope baseline)
- Project documents: project schedule, requirements documentation, risk register, stakeholder register
- Enterprise environmental factors
- Organizational process assets

---

## Tools & Techniques
- Expert judgment
- Data representation (hierarchical charts, responsibility assignment matrix, text-oriented formats)
- Organizational theory
- Meetings

---

## Outputs
- Resource management plan
- Team charter
- Project documents updates (assumption log, risk register)

---

## Key Concepts
- Resources include both team resources (people) and physical resources (materials, equipment, supplies, facilities).
- The resource management plan defines: identification, acquisition, roles and responsibilities, project organization charts, team resource management, training, team development, resource control, and recognition plan.
- Organizational theory informs how people, teams, and organizational units behave; flexible leadership style adapts to team maturity.
- Team charter establishes team values, agreements, and operating guidelines.
- Resource management interacts closely with Schedule and Finance domains.
