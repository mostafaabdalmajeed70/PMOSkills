## Purpose

Define accountability and responsibility assignments, behavioral norms, authority boundaries, and operating commitments for the project team in a single maintained record.

---

## When used

At project setup and when team composition, authority boundaries, or role responsibilities materially change. Updated as the team evolves through the project lifecycle.

---

## Inputs and outputs

### Inputs
- Project charter (authority and named PM)
- Delivery approach and team operating agreement (A09)
- Resource capacity and acquisition record (A26)
- Stakeholder register (A07 — for role boundary clarity)
- Organizational structure and HR policies

### Outputs
- RACI or equivalent accountability matrix
- Role descriptions and authority limits
- Behavioral norms and ground rules
- Escalation and conflict resolution route
- Onboarding reference for new team members

---

## Core fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Role name | Standardized role title used on this project |
| Responsible party | Named person or team assigned to the role |
| Accountability (RACI A) | Who is accountable for the decision or deliverable |
| Consulted (RACI C) | Who must be consulted before the decision |
| Informed (RACI I) | Who must be informed of the outcome |
| Authority limits | What decisions this role can make without escalation |
| Behavioral norms | Ground rules agreed by the team |
| Conflict resolution route | Agreed first-step path for interpersonal or role conflict |
| Escalation path | Who to engage when authority limits are exceeded |

## Tailoring guidance

- A visual RACI matrix is a valid format; keep it to roles, not every task.
- Behavioral norms (team charter content) belong here, not in A09 (which governs delivery cadence and ways of working).
- For small teams, a simple one-page roles-and-norms summary is sufficient.
- Separate from A26 (Resource Capacity and Acquisition Record) — this artifact defines how people work together; A26 tracks staffing levels and acquisition.

---

## Waste test

Fail if a separate team charter, RACI-D sheet, and cross-functional skill inventory each contain partially overlapping responsibility content with no single authoritative source.
